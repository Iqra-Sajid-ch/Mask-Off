Why Mask Off?

Deepfakes and synthetic media are spreading across social platforms, news, and messaging apps. Mask Off uses trained neural networks to scan uploads, sample video frames with MViTv2, and deliver clear Real vs. Manipulated verdicts — so individuals, journalists, and investigators can make informed decisions quickly.

How Detection Works

Every analysis follows the same transparent pipeline — from upload to final verdict.

📤

1. Upload or Capture

Upload a suspicious image or video (MP4, AVI, MOV, WebM). Max 50MB, 1 minute for videos.

🎞️

2. Frame Extraction

Videos are sampled at ~1 frame per second. Full frames are analyzed — no face required.

🧠

3. AI Analysis

Video frames use MViTv2; images use EfficientNet — each scored Real or Manipulated with confidence %.

📊

4. Results & Reports

Majority voting decides the final verdict. Save history, download PDF reports with thumbnails.

Understanding deepfakes and why verification matters.

What are Deepfakes?

Deepfakes use AI to swap faces, alter expressions, or generate entirely synthetic video. They can spread misinformation, impersonate public figures, and enable fraud.

  • Face-swap manipulation in political videos
  • AI-generated profile photos on social media
  • Synthetic voice + video scams

Who Uses Mask Off?

Journalists verifying sources, cybercrime investigators, social media moderators, and anyone who needs a quick authenticity check before sharing media.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is video detection?

We sample ~1 frame/sec and classify full frames with MViTv2, then combine scores with majority voting. Works on any video — faces not required.

Can real videos be flagged as fake?

Our model uses a balanced threshold and majority voting to reduce false positives. Low-quality or heavily compressed footage may affect results — always review per-frame details.

What file formats are supported?

Images: JPG, PNG. Videos: MP4, AVI, MOV, WebM — max 50MB and 1 minute duration.

Are my uploads stored?

Detection results and thumbnails are saved to your account history when logged in. Original files are processed in memory and not permanently stored on the server.
